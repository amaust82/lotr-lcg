# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two distinct profiles sharing the same app:

**New players** — still internalizing the 7-phase sequence and keyword rules. Sitting at a table, cards and tokens spread out, anxiety about doing it wrong. They need guided prompts, plain-English rule reminders, and the confidence that they haven't missed a step.

**Experienced players** — know the flow cold. They need sub-second keyword lookups and a collection they can filter without switching to a spreadsheet. Any extra friction is friction they didn't have before the app existed.

Both profiles use the app at a gaming table, phone in hand, in conversation with other players. Neither can afford to stare at a screen for more than a few seconds.

## Product Purpose

A PWA companion for the Lord of the Rings Living Card Game (FFG/Asmodee). Runs fully offline. Three functions in MVP:

1. **Collection Manager** — track owned cards across core sets, expansions, and cycles; filter and search the full card database.
2. **Turn Guide** — stepthrough of the 7-phase play sequence, with Guided (new player) and Reference (veteran) modes against the same content.
3. **Rules Reference / Keyword Lookup** — searchable index of keywords, timing rules, and edge cases; instant results from a single search box.

Success: a player can open the app mid-session, find what they need, and put the device down inside 10 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Immersive, Atmospheric, Purposeful.

The app should feel like it belongs in the world of the game — dark, deliberate, and rich — without being theatrical or heavy. The tone is the quiet authority of an expert's reference tool, not the spectacle of a fantasy game UI. Emotion: confidence and calm, not wonder or excitement.

Visual reference: the illustrated quick-reference card set for the LOTR LCG — bordered card motif, clean typographic hierarchy, AI-generated fantasy art used sparingly. That balance of structured information and atmospheric texture is the target.

## Anti-references

**Not like Notion or Obsidian.** Too minimal, too document-y. Blank surfaces, no color hierarchy, passive typography. This app needs character and atmosphere. A player who opens it at the table should immediately feel they're in the right place for the game they're playing.

**Not like a mobile game UI.** No glowing buttons, gradient banners, loot-chest energy, or gamified reward loops. This is a tool for a game, not a game itself.

## Design Principles

1. **The game is the game; the app is in the background.** Every design decision defers to the physical experience. The app exists only where the physical game falls short — before sessions, between sessions, and for the occasional mid-play lookup.

2. **Speed is a feature.** A player holding a phone while managing cards and tokens has no patience for choreography. Every interaction must be completable in seconds without breaking conversation. Anything that causes a player to linger is a defect.

3. **Serve both masters without a gate.** Guided mode for new players, Reference mode for veterans — neither flow should be buried behind the other. Density must be available instantly without requiring onboarding.

4. **Atmosphere through constraint.** Dark and immersive without theatrical excess. The LOTR world is carried by disciplined restraint — right material choices, right typographic weight, right density — not decoration or spectacle.

5. **Legibility is non-negotiable.** The primary environment is a dimly lit gaming table. Contrast and readability are never traded away for visual effect. Low-light conditions drive every foreground/background decision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Given the dark theme and gaming-table (low-light) primary environment, contrast headroom above the AA floor is preferred wherever practical. All interactive elements keyboard-accessible. Screen reader support for core flows.
